## What Is Ketamine and How Are Tablets Formulated?
Ketamine, first synthesized in the 1960s, is a dissociative anesthetic with applications in medicine and veterinary care. In tablet form, it's often prescribed for off-label uses like managing treatment-resistant depression or chronic pain. Tablets provide a controlled oral dose, typically ranging from 25mg to 100mg, allowing for easier administration compared to injections.

By 2026, advancements in pharmacology have improved ketamine's bioavailability in oral forms, reducing side effects like nausea. However, it's Schedule III in the US and similarly regulated globally, requiring strict medical oversight.

## Medical Uses of Ketamine Tablets
### Treatment for Depression and Mental Health
Ketamine has gained prominence for its rapid antidepressant effects. Low-dose tablets can alleviate symptoms in patients unresponsive to traditional SSRIs. Studies from 2025 show up to 70% response rates in clinical trials.

### Pain Management Applications
For chronic conditions like fibromyalgia or neuropathic pain, ketamine tablets offer non-opioid relief. They modulate glutamate receptors in the brain, providing analgesia without the addiction risks of opioids.

### Emerging Research in 2026
Ongoing trials explore ketamine's role in PTSD and anxiety disorders. With FDA approvals expanding, more clinics offer supervised tablet regimens.

## Risks and Side Effects of Ketamine Use
While beneficial, ketamine isn't risk-free. Common side effects include dizziness, elevated blood pressure, and hallucinations. Long-term use may lead to bladder issues or dependency.
